Question 2.1
Why are the variables declared as private?

Answer:
Variables are declared as private to protect the data from being accessed or modified directly. This is called encapsulation.

------------------------------------------------------------

Question 2.2
What happens if the variables are declared public?

Answer:
If the variables are public, anyone can access and change the values directly, making the program less secure and harder to control.

------------------------------------------------------------

Question 2.3
Why do we use getters and setters?

Answer:
Getters are used to read the values of private variables, while setters are used to update the values safely. They help maintain data security and control.
